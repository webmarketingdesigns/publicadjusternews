More about the value of a loss

The value of a loss is determined by the cost of repair and the amount your insurance company will pay out for that service. The valuation of your loss will also depend on the cost of materials, labor and any other expenses related to the repair. For example, if you need to replace your roof, the value of the loss would be the amount that it costs to replace it. If you are eligible for additional coverage for a specific type of repair, you may be able to have the rest of the damages paid for. When determining the value of your loss, it is important to keep in mind that the value is not what you think the property is worth, but rather what it would sell for if it were for sale.

The bottom line

The bottom line is that a public insurance adjuster will be inspecting your home or business in preparation for filing a claim. They will look for numerous factors to determine the value of your loss so that the insurance company can settle the claim as quickly as possible. A public adjuster will be looking at the condition of the home, the amount of damage sustained, the amount of time it will take for repairs and the amount that you have set aside in an emergency fund.
