Hurricane Damage: What You Need To Know

 

If you have been affected by Hurricane Irma or Hurricane Maria, it is important to know your rights and what steps you need to take in order to protect your property. Here are some key points to keep in mind:

 

- You have the right to hire a public adjuster. This is an advocate who will help protect your interests during the insurance claim process.

 

- Public adjusters are not representatives of the insurance company, so they will work on behalf of the property owner and not the insurance company.

 

- A public adjuster will help document and maximize your claim. They will also work with you throughout the entire claim process to ensure that all relevant information is collected and processed correctly.

 

- Public adjusters work on a contingency fee basis, which means that they only receive payment if you do win your claim. However, this does not mean that they are working for free - their fees typically range from 10% to 20%.

 

There are a few things that you can do to protect your property in the aftermath of a hurricane. Here are some tips:

 

- Keep all important documents, such as insurance policies, repair estimates, and photos of damage, in a secure location.

 

- Make sure to get an estimate from a qualified contractor or technician to assess the damage and get started on any repairs.

 

- Enclose any trees that may need to be cut down, as these will likely incur additional fees during reconstruction.

 

- Contact your insurance company as soon as possible after the storm has passed in order to file a claim and begin the process of getting reimbursed for damages.

Homeowner Insurance Coverage: What You Need To Know


Homeowner insurance policies typically include coverage for damage caused by earthquakes, windstorms, and other events. In addition, many policies cover damage to your home caused by floods, fire, theft, and vandalism. Homeowners insurance does not usually cover damage from hurricanes or other storms. If your home is damaged as a result of a hurricane, you may be eligible for government assistance. Homeowners insurance does not cover damage caused by mold or water leaks. You may also need to purchase additional insurance in order to cover damage caused by certain types of events. Earthquakes are covered under most homeowner policies if the property is located in an areas considered at high risk for seismic activity.

Business Owner Insurance Coverage: What You Need To Know

As a business owner, you need to know what kind of insurance coverage you have. This includes property loss due to hurricane damage, storm damage, mold damage, water damage, or roof damage. If you experience any of these events and your business is located in an affected area, you should contact a public adjuster. A public adjuster will help you get the settlement you deserve from your insurance company.

 

Business owners ought to have general liability insurance in case someone is injured on company property. This coverage can cover legal expenses related to any injury that occurs on company property, including intentional acts and accidents. Business owners should also have automotive liability insurance to protect themselves financially if someone is injured while driving a company vehicle. If the accident involves a third party, such as a pedestrian or cyclist, the business owner may also need personal injury protection (PIP) coverage.
