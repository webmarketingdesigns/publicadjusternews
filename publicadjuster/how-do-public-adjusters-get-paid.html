How do public adjusters get paid?

When you experience a loss due to a covered event such as a natural disaster, fire, storm or other insured loss, a public insurance adjuster will be hired to help you navigate the process. Depending on where you live and what type of insurance policy you own, there may be several different parties involved in the claims process. When the claim is filed, the insurance company will send the adjuster to meet with you and review your loss. During this meeting, you will discuss the damage and how much it will cost to repair or replace your property. The adjuster will then use this information to prepare a repair estimate. During the repair process, the insurance company will pay the public adjuster directly for their services so they can focus on getting the repairs done as efficiently as possible. Depending on the company and your specific situation, there are different ways that public adjusters are compensated. You will want to make sure that the company has enough cash in their budget to pay the adjuster and complete the repairs without going into debt. You may receive a final settlement check after all repairs have been completed and approved by the insurance company. If your claim is denied completely, you will not receive any money from the adjuster since there was no settlement. There are several types of insurance adjusters who specialize in different areas. Here is a breakdown of what each one does and how you can recognize if yours is a good fit for your situation:

Full-Service Public Insurance Adjuster

A full-service public adjuster is hired to assist with claims under any type of commercial property insurance policy. Full-service public adjusters assist with the claims process, take pictures of your property, write reports, communicate with the insurance company and negotiate with them on your behalf. Full-service public adjusters are hired when there is a significant amount of damage on the property. The adjuster may be hired by the insurance company to handle all the claims within a certain geographic area, or they may be hired by the policyholder. Full-service public adjusters are hired because there may be a large amount of damage to the property that requires extensive repairs. Full-service public adjusters are hired when the insurance company determines that the repairs are going to be more than the policy limit. Full-service public adjusters are hired when the insurance company finds out that you falsified your loss report and pays the adjuster to get you to sign a repair estimate. Full-service public adjusters are hired when the insurance company finds out that the damage was not caused by a covered event and pays the adjuster to not pay the claim.

Bottom line

A public insurance adjuster can assist you with your property damage claim and help you navigate the claims process. Before hiring an insurance company-employed public adjuster, do your research to make sure they are legitimate and experienced. If you don't have a public adjuster, you can hire a professional insurance claims consultant who can help you navigate the claims process. When hiring a public insurance adjuster, make sure you get a written contract that details their services and compensation. Get a written estimate of the costs of the repairs and their timeline. The more information you have before hiring a public insurance adjuster, the better equipped you will be to make the best decision for your situation.
